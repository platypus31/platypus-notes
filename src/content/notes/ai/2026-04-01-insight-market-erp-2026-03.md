# Zero ERP in the S&P 500: Why Inaction May Be Your Best Move

When the risk premium disappears, what should investors do? Howard Marks offers insight on navigating high-valuation markets. As we look at today's S&P 500 environment, we find that the equity risk premium (ERP) is close to zero, a situation that often signals caution in the market.

## Understanding Zero ERP

According to recent data from Neuron, the S&P 500 ERP is hovering near zero as of March 2026. This means that investors are no longer being compensated for taking on additional risk through equity investments, making it a challenging time for traditional value investors. Howard Marks notes in his writings that when valuation multiples reach these levels, they often signal a potential market correction, especially given the current environment.

### Value Investment Periods and Market Cycles

Cornell research highlights that value investment cycles typically oscillate between 5-7 years. This means we are currently at an inflection point where value strategies may face challenges in outperforming growth-oriented approaches. As Neuron insights suggest, this period could be a good time for investors to reevaluate their strategies and perhaps realign with broader market trends.
